Leadership and Administration Roles

Member of Academic Conduct Panel (ACP)

This panel simply investigates all department-wide assignments, exams, and dissertations to check whether there is a plagiarism problem. We use Turnitin and detect problematic submissions and arrange a meeting with students. Having listening students and considering all details, we write a report directly to the head of department for the final decision. Over the years, I have investigated many assignments and I have conducted many meetings (as a chair as well as participants). All those taught me the importance of academic writing and even senior students still do know this issue very well. I used those experiences for my supervised students and so far non of my students had this kind of problem.

Leading Module Review Group

I worked as a member of the Module Review Group and leading responsible for macro and finance modules in the department. The idea of module review was to get a better understanding of the topics that are being taught, cases of significant repetitions, topics that are being missed, topics that could be included etc. Having investigated all module across the department I wrote an advisory report that reviewed the current provision to look at whether there are modules that may no longer be needed, perhaps due to excessive repetition and whether changes in what we offer should be considered

Leading Peer-Teaching Observations

Every year I have been observing (approximately 5 observations) new teaching fellow (PhD) as a member of Peer Observation of Teaching Group over the three years. After each observation, I write a report directly to Deputy of Head of Department. have been also observed but this observing not only beneficial for the observed one but also as a observer I have learned a lot. For example, I realised new and fresh teaching approach that I did not use and not use very effectively. After all those observations, I updated myself too.

Interview Panel Member For Student Recruitment

I was also one of panel members for department wide student job applications and interviews for Warwick Internship Scheme for Economists (WISE). For example, in 2020, With these interviews, we hire students for as a research assistant for research and teaching and/or admistrative jobs.
