    What I have been teaching at Warwick: Teaching Portfolio

    Graduate Levels

    EC916 Topics in Global Finance - Module Leader
    Graduate Level, 150 Students, Since 2019, Feedbacks: 4.75/5

    I have been teaching this module since 2019 and have been a sole lecturer and module leader. I prepared all lecture notes, slides, exams, seminar questions, and solutions. Over the years, I have revised all content and then redesigned and restructured this module in terms of both content and delivery methods. I believe by the help of these improvements, the module became the most popular graduate module in the department. Also, I introduced the Twitter hashtag (#EC916) to increase engagements with students and connection to content out of class. I believe this enhanced student learning a lot and kept them always updated by connecting real-world examples. I have used Vevox for almost every lecture to have more interactive lectures. Also, to get personal feedback and small test, I have used Qualtrics regularly. As much research showed that many macroeconomics textbooks had barely changed and lagged behind the practice of economics, hence I designed this module's notes/slides not depend on textbooks but on recent-updated journal papers. In addition, I always use real-world examples by introducing non-academic journal articles, and I can comfortably say that this is loved by students and increase their motivation since they see that materials touch the real world.

    Undergraduate Levels

    EC106: Quantitative [Macro] Economics - Module Lecturer
    Undergraduate Level, 400 Students, NEW! 2023, Feedbacks:

    This is 1 year module for Maths and Statistics students. In the first term they learn micro and in the second term they will learn macro which I teach alone as a lecturer. I have preapred and redesigned the module. In other words, I prepared everything from zero. This module is mainly for maths and statistics students, and for that reason, I constructed a modern approach by considering evidence/data based curriculum. I divided the module into three parts 1) Theory/Concepts 2) Real World Connection 3) Tools. In the concept part, by using the standard approach, I cover important topics. In the real-world connection part, we look at those concepts in a real-world framework by considering recent/related economic and financial historical events. For the final part, I introduce how those concepts can be applied and replicated again with an empirical approach. For this part, I mainly use the QuantEcon and Our World In Data source and some other materials I prepared by myself.

    EC331: Research in Applied Economics - Module Lecturer
    Undergraduate Level, 12 Students, Since 2019, Feedbacks: 5/5

    Every year I supervise 10-12 BSc Economics / PPE students for their final year dissertations. In this two hours classes (1+1) I teach student what is research question, how to decide research topic properly, give detailed information on the potential research methods, in additon to teach how to deal with data collection and cleaning as well as, have a discussion the potenstion output of this research. I meet my students two times in a week both as a class and one-to-one meeting. Moreover, I have always been keen on my student's further careers; thus, I help them with their job applications and their materials (CV, SoP, Cover Letter etc.) in addition to formal supervising. I have regular meetings with them to improve their CV and redesign the cover letters of my final year students. I have never written a standard/generic reference letter but a very specific and program-oriented letter. Before finalising the letter, I meet my students, learn their career plans, discuss the details of the program, and check their CVs and Cover letters; then, I write a letter. For that reason, I believe many of my previous students are still in contact with me and ask similar questions, too, even after a couple of years since they believe that I am doing this not for the sake of doing it or as a duty.

    Finance in 21st Century-- Module Leader
    Pre-Undergraduate Level, 30 Students, Since 2022, Feedbacks:

    Also, I teach this course at the Warwick Pre-University Summer School for future university students. In this module we have three themes and we discuss and give broad information on the recent developments in these areas. The first themse is Bitcoin, cryptocurrency, central bank digital currency. To investigate this theme we ask the question whether Bitcoin will become the money of the future? The second topic is Global Climate Crises and Financial Stability. Almost all around the world central banks are responsibly of financial stability of their domestic economy. However, in a financially interconnected world climate change bring a fresh challenges which is global and beyond the capacity of many central banks around the world. For that reason, the collective acction is neccesary. We discuss potential problems and solutions ways. Last, we focus on the recent developments in data science and how these technological andvances will affect financial markets and financial decisions. The discussion stays in the big data and algorithm trade frame work.
